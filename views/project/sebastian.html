SYNOPSIS

Sebastian is an 8-year old boy who lives with his grandmother in an ordinary house in an ordinary, monochrome world. But when Sebastian gets on the bike to go for a ride, the adventure into his imagination begins. Mesmerized by the transformation of the colorless city Sebastian crosses the street without paying attention. A car hits him, but only his bike is damaged. After the accident, Sebastian has vivid dreams re-experiencing his birth and entering a fairytale world. When the girl and her mother return the fixed bike, the girl gives Sebastian her magnifying glass. Through this glass everything appears in color, except the newspaper. The kids set the newspaper on fire and run away from the inescapable realities of the adult existence.

About the film maker

Vlad Marsavin, an entrepreneur, has been involved in a number of successful businesses, music and art projects in Europe, Russia and the US since 1993. From 2002 to 2009 he worked in Europe as a music producer. His company, Amiata Records recorded and produced more than a hundred of Italian and international artists, such as Synaulia, Whisky Trail, Aldo Brizzi, Marino De Rosas, Antonio Breschi, Sainkho Namtchylak, Fabio Forte, Fratelli Mancuso, and many others. He graduated from Moscow Aviation Institute with an MSE degree and received Diploma from the Moscow International School of Translation and Interpreting. In addition, Vlad studied film directing at the Los Angeles Film School. Vlad also wrote, directed and produced an animated film “Sebastian”, which was accepted into over 50 film festivals around the world and won many. He has produced documentary “Mama, I’m gonna kill you”, executive produced a short film “Hallucinaut” and recently directed another short titled “Albedo Absolut”, currently in post-production. Other projects in various development stages.
